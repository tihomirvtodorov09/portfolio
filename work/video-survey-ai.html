Video Survey AI

Business Problem

What was costing the business

In-person and video-call surveys don't scale well — each one needs a surveyor's real-time attention, and the resulting report still has to be written up afterwards. As volume grows, that manual step becomes the bottleneck.

My Goal

What I set out to do

Explore whether job footage alone — a walkthrough video, not a live call — could be turned directly into a structured, room-by-room surveyor report, removing the need for a surveyor to be present in real time for every job.

Architecture

How it’s built

A computer vision and AI pipeline that processes removals job footage and generates room-by-room reports, including cubic footage and packing material estimates, with ongoing calibration work to improve accuracy on both fronts.

Implementation

The details that mattered

Currently in active development. The core challenge is calibration — getting cubic footage and packing material estimates to a level of accuracy that's actually usable for pricing, not just directionally correct.

Technology Used

What it’s built with

Computer vision (frame extraction)qwen2.5vl (Ollama)Groq Whisper APIMistral (local, JSON structuring)
Business Outcome

What changed

Business impact is still being validated. I'd rather show this honestly as work in progress than attach a number to it before the accuracy work is done.

Lessons Learned

What I took from it

Computer vision estimation is a harder calibration problem than document-based extraction — a video frame doesn't carry the same explicit structure as a typed inventory list, so the model has to infer far more.

Engineering Decisions

Why footage instead of requiring a live call?
A recorded walkthrough removes the scheduling constraint entirely — the surveyor’s time becomes the bottleneck only for review, not for every single visit.
