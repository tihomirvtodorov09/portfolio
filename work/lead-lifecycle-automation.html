Business Problem

What was costing the business

Leads arrived from four different sources — Compare My Move, KonnectYou, GetAMover, and the company website — and were followed up manually. Response time depended on who was free and how busy the day was. Two of those sources even shared the same subject line ("New Quote Request"), making manual triage slower still.

My Goal

What I set out to do

Build a system that responded to every lead instantly and consistently, regardless of source or time of day — and then went further, re-engaging leads that hadn't converted the first time, rather than letting them disappear after one missed follow-up.

Architecture

How it's built

A Gmail-star-driven state machine built in Google Apps Script. A blue star starts the sequence; a green tick stops it. Source-specific parsers read each lead type, distinguishing GetAMover from the company website by sender address rather than subject line, since both use identical wording. A six-day follow-up sequence runs on top, with Twilio SMS triggered on day zero and after no-answer calls. Office-hours logic shifts leads that arrive late in the day forward by one day, so no one gets a late-night text. Two additional campaign scripts run monthly re-engagement passes: one scans the previous two months of leads for a win-back pass, the other scans the current month's inbound leads but filters by each lead's actual move date, so promotions reach people moving in a specific window regardless of when they first enquired.

Implementation

The details that mattered

PropertiesService tracks send-state across all sources to prevent duplicate sends, with a shared daily send limit so volume stays predictable. A recurring debugging challenge was false-positive reply detection — the script would occasionally think a lead had replied when it hadn't — solved by adding a date filter to Gmail's search query so only genuinely new messages counted as replies. Getting the correct internal label IDs for Apps Script's search syntax took some trial and error, since Gmail's star types aren't documented in an obviously accessible way.

Business Outcome

What changed

Every lead now gets a near-instant, consistent first response regardless of which of the four sources it came from or what time it arrived. Leads that don't convert immediately are re-engaged automatically rather than left cold. Manual admin time per lead dropped, and follow-up no longer depends on who happens to be available that day.

Lessons Learned

What I took from it

Better communication beats simply communicating faster. Speed matters, but the leads that actually convert are the ones who feel like they're dealing with someone consistent and attentive — not just the first company to reply. The hardest part of this build wasn't the automation logic, it was correctly modelling the business's real communication rhythm.

Engineering Decisions

Why Google Apps Script?
It runs natively inside Gmail and Google Workspace, where the leads already live — no separate server or hosting needed for something that just has to watch an inbox and send messages.
Why Twilio for SMS?
Reliable delivery and a straightforward API meant SMS could be added as a fallback channel without building telephony infrastructure from scratch.
