Sales Infrastructure

Sales CRM & Metrics Tracker

A deliberately lightweight CRM, chosen because it was the right-sized tool for the problem, not because it was the most impressive one.

Business Problem
No shared, low-cost way to track leads across salespeople
→
Solution
Lightweight spreadsheet CRM with scripted automation
→
Outcome
Reduced admin, new visibility into metrics
Tooling Cost
Before Paid CRM software
After Free, in-house spreadsheet

System Flow

Lead entry→Templated follow-up→Notes log→Dashboard rollup
Business Problem

What was costing the business

Multiple salespeople needed a shared way to track leads, follow-ups, and basic performance metrics, without the cost or overhead of a full CRM platform.

My Goal

What I set out to do

Build the smallest system that solved the problem — a genuine build-vs-buy decision where "buy" would have meant paying for CRM software the team didn't yet need, and "build big" would have meant over-engineering a problem a well-structured spreadsheet could solve.

Architecture

How it’s built

A spreadsheet-based CRM with templated follow-up emails, a notes logging system, conditional formatting for at-a-glance status, and a simple dashboard rolling up activity across salespeople.

Implementation

The details that mattered

Built with scripted automation triggers, which meant resolving permission scope issues along the way, plus fixing special-character corruption that crept into templated email strings.

Technology Used

What it’s built with

Google SheetsApps Script triggersConditional formattingDashboard rollups
Business Outcome

What changed

Reduced admin overhead for tracking leads across salespeople and gave visibility into metrics that previously weren't tracked at all. It's deliberately simple, and has room to expand as the team's needs grow.

Lessons Learned

What I took from it

Not every problem needs a platform. Choosing the right-sized tool — free, familiar, and fast to build — was the correct call here, and knowing when not to over-engineer is its own kind of judgement.

Engineering Decisions

Why a spreadsheet instead of a custom CRM?
The team didn’t yet have a problem that justified the cost or complexity of a dedicated CRM platform. A well-structured spreadsheet solved the actual problem at zero marginal cost.
